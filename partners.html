Online Physiotherapy Consultations

Aisling Feeley - Physiotherapist & Medicine Student

Why choose online?
In an ever-changing busy word, it’s becoming increasingly difficult to find the time to do the things that matter to us most, including looking after our health. Often injury concerns, aches and niggling injuries get put on the back burner.

Online consultations give you the same expert advice you would expect with in-person sessions, with the added bonus of scheduling at a time that suits your busy lifestyle. Whether you’re looking for injury rehab advice, a second opinion or just to pick the brainsof an experienced chartered physiotherapist, you’re covered.

My approach to treatment never changes. I want to educate you and support you on your road to recovery, no matter how big or small your concern may be. Together we’ll work to optimise your health, performance and overall quality of life.

Why I choose Physiotherapy?

Coming from a sports-mad household, the human body and performance science always interested me. Combined with my love of working with people, Physiotherapy was the perfect option for me.

My Experience

I graduated from University College Dublin in 2017 with a Bachelor of Science (Hons) in Physiotherapy. I completed over 1,000 hours of clinical placement during my four years in UCD, working across a wide variety of settings including adult ICU, paediatric ICU, care of the elderly and neurology.

After graduating, I worked initially in Primary Care in Roscommon, treating patients of all ages in the Community setting. I then secured a job in a busy South Dublin Hospital. Here I was fortunate enough to gain experience in stroke rehab, acute injury, respiratory physiotherapy and care of the elderly. I also trained in Cardiac Rehabilitation, delivering exercise classes for patients after heart surgery/events.

I caught the travel bug in October 2018 and moved to New Zealand for 10 months. After obtaining my New Zealand Physiotherapy Registration, I began working in two busy West Auckland private practices. The experience I gained in New Zealand has been invaluable to my physio career as I had the opportunity to work with both elite athletes and members of the general population. After a fantastic time abroad working and travelling, I returned to Ireland in August 2019 after receiving a place to study Graduate Entry Medicine at the University of Limerick. This past year I’ve balanced my Medical studies with a new job, working part-time as a Chartered Physiotherapist in a Limerick city private practice.

Online Nutrition Consultations

Evan Lynch - Nutritionist

Evan is our resident nutritionist having proven himself through his work with many top Irish athletes both privately and through Cycling and Athletics Ireland. Evan has completed a B. Sc in Food Science & Health, a P.G Diploma in dietetics, and is currently finishing up his Master’s degree in Sports & Exercise nutrition. With a past as an international track and field athlete, Evan knows the science and also has the perspective of the athlete to draw upon.

“Since working with Evan my approach to food and fueling my workouts and racing has totally changed for the better. In the past my relationship with food was poor as I was always under fueling workouts in order to be as light as possible but this came with a negative effect to my performance, I lost a huge amount of weight but with that came a loss of power and cost me a huge number of results on the bike. Evan has helped me achieve the ideal race weight and has helped me change the way I think about food. I can safely say that without the help of Evan I would still be stuck in a rut when it comes to fueling my workouts and I would most definitely be in a constant battle with the weighing scales. Today too many young cyclists are starving themselves in order to achieve a high power to weight ratio which his having a detrimental effect on both their performances and mental health. I was once one of these athletes and I can now safely say that it’s a thing of the past since.”
- Daire Feeley
